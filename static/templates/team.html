Outfluenza Team

Over the past few decades, the world has witnessed a revolution in information technology. With developments such as personal computers, smartphones, and tablets, technology is being used to obtain, disseminate, and share information as never before. Moreover, advances in data analytics have given us powerful tools to organize the information available to us and draw meaningful conclusions from them. Most importantly, technology and data have become essential to public health by creating efficiency in medicine.

E-prescriptions allows patients to obtain quicker access to the medicines and pharmaceuticals, and telemedicine has optimized medical appointments through videoconferencing and teleconferencing thereby reducing the number of face-to-face interactions needed. This not only creates efficiency, but also enables bringing modern medicine to people who wouldn’t have had access to it otherwise. Mobile devices, for example, can be used in remote villages to call for help in the event of a difficult labor or procedure.

Handheld devices are often used by healthcare workers to collect and collate survey data, and to transfer data to a central location in real time. Databases and big data storage allows to store millions of data points about patients and their heartbeat, blood content, condition, etc. We intend on using all the data that is collected and conduct statistical analysis and evaluate and compare the effectiveness of different treatments, as well as follow the spread of influenza across populations. We aim to leverage machine learning and other tools to analyze different trends and predict outbreaks before they happen.
